In D&D 5th Edition, the alignment system is a way to describe a character's moral and ethical outlook, providing a general guideline for their behavior and decision-making. Here's a brief explanation of the alignment system: Alignment has two aspects: a character's moral alignment (Lawful, Neutral, or Chaotic) and their ethical alignment (Good, Neutral, or Evil). These aspects combine to form nine possible alignments:
  • Lawful Good (LG): Characters with this alignment value order, justice, and the greater good. They strive to uphold laws and act in a way that benefits others.
  • Neutral Good (NG): Characters with this alignment seek to do good but may not be bound by strict rules. They prioritize kindness, compassion, and doing what they believe is right.
  • Chaotic Good (CG): Characters with this alignment value personal freedom and individuality. They act on their own principles and often rebel against authority if they perceive it as oppressive.
  • Lawful Neutral (LN): Characters with this alignment believe in order and structure. They adhere to laws and regulations but may prioritize maintaining the system over individual moral judgments.
  • True Neutral (TN): Characters with this alignment maintain a balance between good and evil, law and chaos. They often act in their own self-interest or based on circumstances rather than adhering to strict moral or ethical codes.
  • Chaotic Neutral (CN): Characters with this alignment value personal freedom and individual choice above all else. They are unpredictable, following their own whims and desires without regard for rules or conventions.
  • Lawful Evil (LE): Characters with this alignment believe in using laws and systems to further their own evil ends. They are often manipulative and seek power and control over others.
  • Neutral Evil (NE): Characters with this alignment prioritize their own interests and desires, often without regard for the well-being of others. They act selfishly and opportunistically.
  • Chaotic Evil (CE): Characters with this alignment revel in chaos and destruction. They seek personal gratification and inflict pain and suffering on others without remorse.
    • It's important to note that alignment is a guideline and not an absolute determinant of a character's actions. Characters can exhibit traits and behaviors that don't perfectly align with their stated alignment, and their alignment may evolve or change throughout their adventures. Alignment can be a useful tool for players and Dungeon Masters to understand a character's moral compass and motivations, but ultimately, it's up to the players to bring their characters to life and make decisions that reflect their personality, background, and beliefs.
