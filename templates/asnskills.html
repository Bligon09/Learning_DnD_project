Skills represent training that your character has had, and something that they are talented in. If you have a proficiency in a skill, that means if the DM ask for a roll with that skill, you get to add your proficiency bonus You already get to skills based on your background, now you get to choose skills based on your race, and class. Below is some more info about skills, but they are mostly exactly what their name suggests.

In Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition (D&D 5e), skills represent a character's proficiency and expertise in various areas. Here's a brief description of all the skills in the game: Acrobatics: This skill reflects a character's agility, balance, and coordination. It is used for performing acrobatic maneuvers, avoiding falls or obstacles, and maintaining balance in precarious situations.
Animal Handling: Animal Handling represents a character's ability to interact with and understand animals. It is used for calming or controlling animals, handling mounts, or interpreting their behavior.
Arcana: Arcana represents a character's knowledge and understanding of magic, spells, and magical creatures. It is used for identifying magical items, recalling lore about spells or magical phenomena, and deciphering magical writings.
Athletics: Athletics measures a character's physical strength and athletic prowess. It is used for feats of strength, climbing, swimming, jumping, or engaging in physically demanding activities.
Deception: Deception measures a character's ability to convincingly lie, trick, or mislead others. It is used for disguises, bluffing, forging documents, or maintaining a false identity.
History: History represents a character's knowledge of past events, civilizations, and historical figures. It is used for recalling information about historical events, ancient ruins, or understanding the significance of historical artifacts.
Insight: Insight reflects a character's ability to read people's intentions and emotions. It is used for detecting lies, assessing someone's trustworthiness, or understanding unspoken messages through body language.
Intimidation: Intimidation measures a character's ability to instill fear or exert dominance over others. It is used for intimidating or coercing individuals, making threats, or asserting authority.
Investigation: Investigation represents a character's ability to scrutinize and analyze details to uncover clues or hidden information. It is used for searching for secret doors, finding hidden objects, or piecing together information from a crime scene.
Medicine: Medicine reflects a character's knowledge and proficiency in treating injuries and ailments. It is used for stabilizing dying characters, providing first aid, diagnosing illnesses, or administering antidotes.
Nature: Nature represents a character's understanding of the natural world, including animals, plants, and geography. It is used for identifying creatures, tracking, foraging, or recalling information about the natural environment.
Perception: Perception measures a character's awareness and keenness of the senses. It is used for detecting hidden objects, spotting traps, noticing details, or eavesdropping.
Performance: Performance reflects a character's skill in entertaining an audience through artistic expression. It is used for acting, singing, playing a musical instrument, or any other form of performance art.
Persuasion: Persuasion measures a character's ability to influence others through charm, diplomacy, or negotiation. It is used for convincing NPCs, negotiating deals, or swaying attitudes through reasoned arguments.
Religion: Religion represents a character's knowledge of deities, religious practices, and religious texts. It is used for recalling information about religious rituals, identifying religious symbols, or understanding religious customs.
Sleight of Hand: Sleight of Hand measures a character's dexterity and nimbleness in performing manual tricks or manipulating objects covertly. It is used for pickpocketing, concealing small objects, or performing tricks with cards or coins.
Stealth: Stealth represents a character's ability to move silently and remain unnoticed. It is used for sneaking, hiding, moving silently, or avoiding detection.
Survival: Survival reflects a character's ability to live off the land, navigate in the wilderness, and find sustenance.
